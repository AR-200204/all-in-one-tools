Why Use a Yes/No Generator?

Analysis paralysis is a real phenomenon. When faced with two relatively equal choices, or when you are simply unsure about a course of action, your brain can get potential stuck in a loop of overthinking. This tool acts as an external circuit breaker.

The Psychology of Random Decisions

Interestingly, often the moment the answer is revealed, you realize what you actually wanted. If the Oracle says "Yes" and you feel disappointed, then you know your true desire was "No". In this way, a random generator serves not just as a decision maker, but as a clarity tool.

Common Uses

  • Deciding on trivial matters: Should I order pizza? Should I go for a walk?
  • Breaking ties: When a group is split 50/50.
  • Gaming: As a replacement for a coin flip.
